
# 🛑 FRC Bumpers: Construction & Rules
> **The First Line of Defense:** Bumpers aren't just an aesthetic requirement; they are a critical engineering component. A well-built set of bumpers protects our robot, complies with strict FIRST rules, and makes Team 2064 look professional on the field.

---

## 🛡️ Why Bumpers Matter
Bumpers serve three main purposes in the FIRST Robotics Competition:
1.  **Robot Protection:** They absorb massive impacts during high-speed collisions, protecting our delicate electronics, XRP prototypes, and custom CNC'd mechanisms.
2.  **Field Protection:** They prevent our metal chassis from gouging the field perimeter or damaging field elements.
3.  **Alliance Identification:** They clearly display our team number and alliance color (Red or Blue) to the referees and the audience.

---

## 📏 The "Golden Rules" of Bumpers
!!! danger "Strict Inspection Item"
    Bumpers are heavily scrutinized during Robot Inspection. If our bumpers fail inspection, **the robot does not play**. Always verify these numbers against the *current year's FRC Game Manual*.

* **Wood Backing:** Must be constructed from **3/4-inch** thick (nominal) plywood or solid, robust wood. (No particle board).
* **Height:** Bumpers typically must be exactly **5 inches** tall (verify tolerance in the current manual).
* **Bumper Foam:** The rules have shifted! We no longer use standard pool noodles. You must use approved **Foam Profiles** (such as specific EPE, EVA, or PE foam) that meet the density, dimensional, and cross-section requirements outlined in the manual.
* **Fabric:** Must be a rugged, smooth cloth. (We standardly use 1000 Denier Cordura).
* **Weight Limit:** The entire bumper assembly has a strict weight limit (usually around **15 lbs**, verify in current manual).

## 🛠️ Materials & Tools Required

=== "Lumber & Padding"
    * 3/4" Plywood (cut to 5" height)
    * Approved Bumper Foam Profiles (e.g., D-profile or stacked foam meeting current specs)
    * Gaffer tape or duct tape (for securing foam)

=== "Fabric & Fasteners"
    * 1000D Cordura Fabric (Red and Blue, or Reversible)
    * Heavy-duty staples (3/8" or 1/2")
    * Wood glue

=== "Mounting Hardware"
    * 1/4-20 T-Nuts (embedded in the wood)
    * 1/4-20 Bolts or Quick-Release Pins
    * Custom aluminum angle brackets (machined in-shop)

=== "Shop Tools"
    * Bandsaw or Table Saw
    * Pneumatic Staple Gun
    * Cordless Drill & Driver
    * Clamps

## 🏗️ Step-by-Step Construction

### 1. The Wood Backing
Measure your robot perimeter exactly. Cut the 3/4" plywood into 5-inch tall strips. 
!!! warning "Pro-Tip: Leave a Gap"
    Always leave a small tolerance (about 1/8" to 1/4") between the wood bumper segments and the robot frame so the fabric has room to wrap without making the bumpers impossible to slide on.

### 2. Install Mounting Hardware (Do this FIRST!)
**Do not attach the foam yet.** Drill the holes for your mounting brackets and hammer in your T-Nuts on the *outside* face of the wood (the side the foam will cover). If you staple the fabric first, you will not be able to install the mounts properly.

### 3. Attach the Bumper Foam
Position your approved foam profiles against the wood backing, ensuring they cover the required vertical span. 
* Use gaffer tape or masking tape to bind the foam securely to the wood backing. 
* **Corners:** First cuts matter! Miter the ends of the foam at 45-degree angles so they meet perfectly at the corners of the robot. Ensure there are no unprotected gaps.

### 4. Wrap the Fabric
Lay the Cordura fabric face-down on a clean table. Place the wood/foam assembly on top.
1. Pull the fabric tightly over the foam to the back of the wood.
2. Staple the center first, pulling the fabric taut as you move outward.
3. **The Corners:** Treat the corners like wrapping a present. Fold the excess fabric neatly, ensure it doesn't bulge beyond the thickness limit, and staple heavily.

## 🔩 Mounting Strategies
*How we attach the bumpers to the chassis is just as important as how we build them.*

??? info "Method A: Two Sets of Bumpers (Standard)"
    We build one complete RED set and one complete BLUE set. 
    **Pros:** Very durable, looks incredibly clean.
    **Cons:** Costs twice as much in materials, takes up more space in the pit, and takes time to swap bolts between matches.

??? info "Method B: Reversible Bumpers"
    We build one set of wood and foam, but the fabric has a sewn-in flap with velcro that flips between red and blue.
    **Pros:** Lightning-fast color changes between matches. Cheaper on wood and hardware.
    **Cons:** Much harder to sew. If the velcro gets dirty or wears out, the flap sags.

!!! success "The Panther Project Standard"
    We prefer to use **heavy-duty quick-release pins** (like ball-lock pins) through custom aluminum brackets over standard bolts. It allows the drive team to swap or remove bumpers in seconds without carrying a wrench to the field.

## 🔢 Team Numbers
FRC rules dictate exactly how **2064** must appear on our robot.

* **Size:** The numbers must be at least **4 inches tall** with a **1/2-inch stroke** (thickness).
* **Color:** The numbers must be **White** (or have a white outline).
* **Placement:** The number must be clearly visible from all 4 sides of the robot.
* **Application:** We utilize our shop's vinyl cutter or create custom heat-transfer decals to ensure the numbers do not peel off during heavy defense.
