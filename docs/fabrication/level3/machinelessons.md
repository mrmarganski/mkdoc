🛠️ Skill Check: Precision Hub & Axle
> **Objective:** Demonstrate precision turning on the lathe, square milling on the Bridgeport, and accurate hole placement to create a concentric, mated assembly.

---

## 🚦 Phase 1: The Axle (Lathe Operations)
**Material:** 1/2" Hex Shaft

1.  **Cut to Length:** Use the **Milwaukee Bandsaw** to cut a ~3" section of hex shaft. 
    * *Reasoning:* Always leave "extra" material (stock) so the lathe can face the ends perfectly flat and square.
2.  **Face the End:** Mount the shaft in the lathe chuck and "face" the end until it is perfectly flat.
3.  **Turn the Diameter:** Use a turning tool to reduce the hex to a **0.500" Outer Diameter (O.D.)** for exactly **1.000" of length**.
    * *Tip:* Use **Calipers** frequently. As you approach 0.510", take smaller passes (e.g., 0.002") to avoid "undershooting" the fit.

---

## 🏗️ Phase 2: The Hub & Cross-Drill (Milling Operations)
**Material:** 2" x 2" x 1/2" Aluminum Square

1.  **Square the Block:** Mount the plate in the **Milling Machine** vise with parallel standoffs. Use an endmill to "square" the edges so they are 90° to one another.
2.  **Find Center & Bore:** Use an **Edge Finder** to locate the exact center of the 2"x2" face. Drill a pilot hole, then use a **1/2" Drill Bit** to create the center hole.
3.  **Transition: Cross-Drilling the Shaft:** You must now **switch the Hex Shaft from the lathe to the milling machine** to drill the locking hole through the side.
    * **Setup:** Place the hex shaft in the mill vise using vise parallels to keep it level.
    * **Location:** Use the edge finder to locate the end of the shaft. Move the table to **locate the hole exactly 0.250" (1/4") from the lathed end**.
    * **Execution:** Drill a #21 clearance hole completely through the 0.500" diameter section.
    * *Reasoning:* Drilling this on the mill ensures the hole is perfectly centered and perpendicular, which is nearly impossible to do by hand.
      
---

## 🔩 Phase 3: Assembly & Lock
1.  **The Mate:** Slide the lathed end of the shaft into the aluminum hub.
2.  **The Stand-off:** Use **10-32 Washers** on the backside of the block to ensure the nut does not hit the aluminum block directly.
3.  **The Lock:** Align the side hole of the shaft with the hub and secure the assembly with a 10-32 bolt.

---

## 📊 Evaluation Rubric (Mentor Review)

| Category | Emerging (1 pt) | Proficient (2 pts) | Mastery (3 pts) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lathe Precision** | Diameter is ±0.010"; surface finish is rough. | Diameter is within ±0.003" of 0.500". | Diameter is 0.500" (+0.000 / -0.001"); mirror finish. |
| **Mill Accuracy** | Center hole is visibly off-center. | Center hole is within ±0.010" of center. | Center hole is within ±0.002" of true center. |
| **Side Hole Location** | Hole is more than 1/16" off the 0.250" mark. | Hole is within ±0.010" of the 0.250" mark. | Hole is exactly 0.250" from the end; perfectly centered. |
| **The Fitment** | Shaft is too loose or requires a press. | Shaft slides into the block with minor friction. | **Precision Slip Fit:** Smooth entry with zero slop. |
| **Mechanical Sympathy** | Metal chips left in assembly; washers missing. | Assembly is clean; correct hardware used. | Deburred edges; all tool marks removed; perfect torque. |
