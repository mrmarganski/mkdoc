# 🛠️ Skill Check: Precision Hub & Axle
> **Objective:** Demonstrate precision turning on the lathe, square milling on the Bridgeport, and accurate hole placement to create a concentric, mated assembly.

---

## 🚦 Phase 1: The Axle (Lathe Operations)
**Material:** 1/2" Hex Shaft

1.  **Cut to Length:** Use the **Milwaukee Cordless Bandsaw** to cut a ~3" section of hex shaft. 
    * *Reasoning:* Always leave "extra" material (stock) so the lathe can face the ends perfectly flat and square.
2.  **Face the End:** Mount the shaft in the lathe chuck and "face" the end until it is perfectly flat.
3.  **Turn the Diameter:** Use a turning tool to reduce the hex to a **0.500" Outer Diameter (O.D.)** for exactly **1.000" of length**.
    * *Tip:* Use **Calipers** frequently. As you approach 0.510", take smaller passes (e.g., 0.002") to avoid "undershooting" the fit.
4.  **Drill Center Hole:** Use a **Center Drill** followed by a **#21 Drill Bit** to create a 10-32 clearance hole 1" deep into the center of the lathed end.
    * *Reasoning:* Center drills are rigid and prevent the longer, flexible drill bit from "walking" off-center.



---

## 🏗️ Phase 2: The Hub (Milling Operations)
**Material:** 2" x 2" x 1/2" Aluminum Square

1.  **Square the Block:** Mount the plate in the **Milling Machine** vise. Use an endmill to "square" the edges so they are 90° to one another.
    * *Tip:* Use a machinist square to verify your work between setups.
2.  **Find Center:** Use an **Edge Finder** to locate the exact center of the 2"x2" face. 
    * *Reasoning:* In robotics, "eyeballing" center leads to vibrating mechanisms. Precise X/Y coordinates are required.
3.  **Bore the Hole:** Drill a pilot hole, then use a **1/2" Drill Bit** to create the center hole.
    * *Tip:* This is a **Slip Fit**. If your lathing was exactly 0.500", the shaft should slide in with no "wobble" but without needing a hammer.



---

## 🔩 Phase 3: Assembly & Lock
1.  **The Mate:** Slide the lathed end of the shaft into the aluminum hub.
2.  **The Stand-off:** Use **10-32 Washers** on the backside of the block.
    * *Reasoning:* This "stands off" the hardware so the nut does not hit the aluminum block directly, ensuring the clamping force is applied to the shaft.
3.  **The Lock:** Secure the assembly with a 10-32 bolt through the shaft.

## 📊 Evaluation Rubric (Mentor Review)

| Category | Emerging (1 pt) | Proficient (2 pts) | Mastery (3 pts) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lathe Precision** | Diameter is ±0.010"; surface finish is rough or "threaded." | Diameter is within ±0.003" of 0.500"; length is accurate. | Diameter is 0.500" (+0.000 / -0.001"); mirror-like finish. |
| **Mill Accuracy** | Center hole is visibly off-center or block is not square. | Center hole is within ±0.010" of center; edges are square. | Center hole is within ±0.002" of true center; perfect 90° edges. |
| **The Fitment** | Shaft is too loose (rattles) or too tight (requires a press). | Shaft slides into the block with minor friction; no significant play. | **Precision Slip Fit:** Shaft slides in smoothly with zero perceptible "slop." |
| **Mechanical Sympathy** | Metal chips (swarf) left in assembly; washers missing. | Assembly is clean; correct hardware and stand-off logic used. | Deburred edges; all tool marks removed; hardware torqued perfectly. |
| **Tool Protocol** | Used unauthorized machinery or left tools messy. | Used only allowed tools; returned tools to correct locations. | Workstation was cleaner than found; tools returned to foam cutouts. |
