# {{ m1_4_8 }}

Students will show an understanding of tools used and demonstrate their understanding...


* Students have to pick correct tool for each operation
* 3D Print a shape (Premade)  The 3D Print has a hole pattern of 4/5 holes - 10/32 and maybe a tapped hole insert in the print.  It will act as the "JIG/Fixture"
* Students get 5min to make a "drawing of the part they need to cut to fit the jig and the hole sizes and locations" - They can't touch the 3D print after the 5min until they finish this task:
* Students cut a length of material (1x2 tube) - have to measure and cut straight (no milling allowed) and fit in the "jig"
* They also have to drill with hand drill the 4/5 holes into the tube so that the correct fastener attached them in the correct spot.
* At the end of making it, they get the jig back and determine hole alignment and choose the best fastener to attach the print to the jig with screws and nuts accordingly.
* Then tighten the fasteners without crushing the tube or cracking the 3D print, but securing the part from being loose.
