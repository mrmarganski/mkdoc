# 🏭 Fabrication Certification Overview
> **Objective:** Understand the four-tier certification system required to safely operate hand tools, power tools, and heavy machinery in the Team 2064 shop.

Safety is the absolute highest priority on The Panther Project. Because an FRC shop contains everything from simple screwdrivers to industrial CNC routers, we do not grant blanket access to the shop. Instead, tool access is earned through a four-level certification system.

!!! info "Who takes what?"
    * **Levels 1 & 2:** Mandatory for **all** rookies and team members during Phase 1 onboarding. You cannot participate in the build season without these.
    * **Levels 3 & 4:** Reserved for Phase 2. These are advanced modules specifically for members specializing in the **Fabrication Pathway**.

---

## 🟢 Level 1: General Shop 
**Target:** All Members (Phase 1 Requirement)  
Before you pick up a power tool, you must know how to measure accurately and use basic hand tools without stripping bolts or injuring yourself.

* 📏 **[Measurement & Layout](level-1/measurement.md):** Reading calipers, tape measures, and marking parts.
* 🔧 **[Hand Tools](level-1/hand-tools.md):** Wrenches, screwdrivers, Allen keys, and proper torque.
* 🧰 **[Bumper & Pit Tools](level-1/pit-essentials.md):** The specific tools needed to assemble bumpers and maintain the competition pit.

---

## 🟡 Level 2: Power Tools & Basics
**Target:** All Members (Phase 1 Requirement)  
Once you have mastered hand tools, you will learn to safely operate portable power tools, benchtop stations, and basic electronics fabrication.

* 🔋 **[Drills & Impacts](level-2/drills.md):** Safely operating cordless drills and impact drivers.
* 🗜️ **[Benchtop Machines](level-2/bench-machines.md):** Drill press, bench grinder, and belt sander safety.
* 🖨️ **[FDM 3D Printing](level-2/bambu-flashforge.md):** Operating the Bambu Lab X1C and Flashforge printers.
* 🔌 **[Soldering & Wiring](level-2/electronics.md):** Safe soldering practices and basic electrical connections.

---

## 🟠 Level 3: Stationary Woodworking & Advanced Materials
**Target:** Fabrication Pathway Specialists (Phase 2)  
This level introduces heavy, stationary machinery that requires extreme focus and situational awareness, as well as handling toxic materials.

* 🪚 **[Band Saw & Miter Saw](level-3/saws.md):** Safe cutting techniques for aluminum, polycarb, and wood.
* 🪵 **[Table Saw Safety](level-3/table-saw.md):** Ripping materials safely, avoiding kickback, and using push sticks.
* 🧪 **[SLA Resin Printing (Formlabs)](level-3/formlabs-safety.md):** Handling toxic resin, utilizing the Form 3+, and operating the washing/curing stations.

---

## 🔴 Level 4: Digital Fabrication
**Target:** Fabrication Pathway Specialists (Phase 2)  
The highest level of shop certification. You will learn to translate digital CAD models into physical parts using computer-controlled machinery.

* ⚡ **[Laser Engraving](level-4/laser.md):** Operating the Full Spectrum Hobby 40W laser, ventilation, and material safety (what NOT to cut).
* ⚙️ **[CNC Routing](level-4/cnc-routing.md):** CAM setups, feeds/speeds, and operating the Stepcraft Q.408 and Lowrider LR4 for custom aluminum and polycarb parts.

---

## ✅ How to Get Certified

For every tool or machine listed above, you must complete a strict three-step process before you are allowed to use it independently:

1. **Read the Documentation:** Read the specific tool's page in this manual.
2. **Pass the Written Quiz:** Complete the safety check for that specific level.
3. **Pass the Practical:** Physically demonstrate your ability to set up, safely use, and clean up the machine while supervised by a Mentor or the Fabrication Lead.
