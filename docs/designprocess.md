# ⚙️ The Engineering Design Process
> **How We Build:** Whether we are designing a custom XRP ping-pong launcher or a 125lb competition robot, we follow this systematic approach. Great robots aren't built on the first try; they are the result of rigorous engineering.

---

## 1. Define the Problem :material-bullseye-arrow:
* **Identify the Need:** Understand and articulate the exact problem or need that requires a solution.
* **Problem Statement:** Create a clear and concise problem statement that outlines the challenge.
* **Constraints and Criteria:** Determine the constraints (limitations like weight, budget, or dimensions) and criteria (what makes it successful).

## 2. Research and Gather Information :material-book-search:
* **Background Research:** Conduct thorough research on the problem, including existing solutions, scientific principles, and relevant technologies (e.g., watching past FRC game reveals).
* **Stakeholder Input:** Gather information from stakeholders (drive team, mentors, strategists) to understand their needs and preferences.

## 3. Specify Requirements :material-format-list-checks:
* **Functional Requirements:** Define exactly what the solution must do (its functions and features).
* **Non-functional Requirements:** Specify performance standards, reliability, safety, cost, and other non-functional aspects.
* **Design Criteria:** Establish the exact criteria that the final design must meet to pass inspection and be successful.

## 4. Brainstorm and Generate Ideas :material-head-lightbulb:
!!! tip "No Bad Ideas in Brainstorming"
    During this phase, throw everything at the whiteboard. A crazy idea might just be the spark that leads to the winning mechanism.

* **Idea Generation:** Brainstorm multiple potential solutions. Encourage creativity and consider a wide range of possibilities.
* **Team Collaboration:** Work in sub-teams to combine diverse perspectives and expertise.

## 5. Evaluate and Select Solutions :material-scale-balance:
* **Concept Evaluation:** Assess each idea against the defined requirements and constraints from Step 3.
* **Feasibility Analysis:** Consider factors such as technical feasibility, cost, time, and shop resources (can we make this on our CNC or 3D printers?).
* **Decision Matrix:** Use a scoring system to objectively compare and prioritize solutions.

## 6. Develop and Prototype Solutions :material-printer-3d:
!!! warning "Fail Fast, Iterate Quickly"
    Don't build the final version out of aluminum on day one. Prototype with cardboard, wood, or PLA first to prove the geometry works.

* **Detailed Design:** Develop detailed designs for the selected solution in CAD (Onshape), including drawings, specifications, and schematics.
* **Prototype Creation:** Build functional prototypes or models to test the design concept.
* **Testing and Iteration:** Test the prototypes to identify issues and refine the design through cycles of modification.

## 7. Test and Evaluate :material-test-tube:
* **Performance Testing:** Conduct rigorous testing to ensure the solution meets all functional and non-functional requirements.
* **User Testing:** Involve the drive team and end-users in testing to gather feedback and validate usability and effectiveness.
* **Analyze Results:** Analyze the test results to identify any remaining issues, weak points, or areas for improvement.

## 8. Refine and Optimize :material-chart-line:
* **Design Refinement:** Make necessary adjustments and optimizations to the CAD and physical model based on testing feedback and performance data.
* **Cost-Benefit Analysis:** Reevaluate the solution in terms of weight, efficiency, and effectiveness.

## 9. Implement and Produce :material-factory:
* **Final Design:** Finalize the design and prepare detailed documentation for final manufacturing.
* **Production Planning:** Develop a plan for manufacturing, assembly, and wiring.
* **Implementation:** Begin final production and assembly of the mechanism or robot.

## 10. Communicate and Document :material-file-document-edit:
* **Documentation:** Create comprehensive documentation of the design process, including specifications, test results, and user manuals (like this MkDocs site!).
* **Present Findings:** Communicate the results and benefits to the team, judges (Impact/Engineering Awards), and sponsors.

## 11. Monitor and Evaluate :material-eye-check:
* **Monitor Performance:** Continuously monitor the robot's performance in real-world competition conditions.
* **Maintenance and Updates:** Plan for regular pit maintenance and design updates to address any emerging issues between matches.
* **Feedback Loop:** Collect feedback from the drive team and scouts to inform future design cycles for the next season.
